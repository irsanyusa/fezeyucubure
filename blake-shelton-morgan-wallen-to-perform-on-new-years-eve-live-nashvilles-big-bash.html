    Blake Shelton, Morgan Wallen to Perform on 'New Year's Eve Live: Nashville's Big Bash'

    TALENTRECAP.COM Entertainment Celebrity Reading Time: 2 minutes Country superstars Blake Shelton and Morgan Wallen have been invited to perform on New Years Eve Live: Nashvilles Big Bash. The two will grace the program alongside other crooners in the star-studded list.

    09 Aug 2024, 00:00

    TALENTRECAP.COM Entertainment Celebrity

    Photo by: Tyler Golden/NBC

    Reading Time: 2 minutes

    Country superstars Blake Shelton and Morgan Wallen have been invited to perform on New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash. The two will grace the program alongside other crooners in the star-studded list.

    Blake Shelton, Morgan Wallen, More Added to New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash

    On Wednesday, a string of new performers for the holiday special was announced. Aside from Shelton and Wallen, Trace Adkins, Grace Bowers, Kane Brown, Jackson Dean, HARDY, Cody Johnson, Parker McCollum, Megan Moroney, Brothers Osborne, Jon Pardi, Carly Pearce, Trombone Shorty, and Bailey Zimmerman. These singers will join the previously announced line-up of Old Dominion, King, Thomas Rhett, Lynyrd Skynyrd and Lainey Wilson.

    New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash is heading North to New York City spark magic in the beginning of 2024 from the Big Apple and Nashville. Elle King will host the Nashville part of the event along with Entertainment Tonight’s Rachel Smith. Meanwhile, radio host Cody Alan and CBS anchor Lonnie Quinn and Dustin Lynch will be reporting from New York City’s Times Square.

    The five-hour broadcast will consist of 50 performances from Nashville, along with dual countdowns to midnight. Furthermore, the event will feature spectacular collaborations, with artists inviting special guests and artist friends to perform together throughout the night. Crooners will be belting out their biggest hits and will do renditions of some of the most iconic country songs.

    New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash will air live on Sunday, December 31, 7:30-10:00 PM, ET/PT, 10:30 PM-1:05 AM, ET/PT on the CBS Television Network. The program will also stream on Paramount.

    Shelton is Hoping to “Cut Back or Stop Drinking Altogether” in the New Year

    In an interview with Entertainment Tonight, Shelton revealed that reducing his drinking habits is still his goal next year. The country crooner stated that he’s had that New Year’s resolution for years.

    “I haven’t managed to stop drinking yet,” he said. “Even cutting back has been hard. I mean, it’s a resolution though. And I’ll say it again right now — that’s my New Year’s resolution. To either cut back or stop drinking altogether. Let’s just say I said it.”

    Of what he and his wife Gwen Stefani are planning for the New Year’s Eve, Shelton said they’ll be hitting the hay early.

    “If we’re at home during New Year’s Eve, we’re probably going to bed before it even happens,” he said.
