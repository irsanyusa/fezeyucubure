    There are different types of attorneys in contemporary Nigeria. Similarly, various institutions of higher learning offer law programmes. You should determine the best law university in Nigeria based on the type of attorney you wish to become in the future.

    Best law university in Nigeria: Top 20 institutions

    Many prospective attorneys want to know the best law schools in Nigeria. If you are one of them, fret not. Below is a compilation of 20 top institutions that offer quality law education.

    20. The University of Abuja

    • Location: Abuja
    • Year of establishment: 1988
    • Type: Public (federal)
    • Motto: For Unity and Scholarship

    The University of Abuja is one of the federal universities in Nigeria and is ranked among the best law universities in Nigeria. The institution has since grown since its inception and is known for offering world-class education. It has a Faculty of Law known for producing competent graduates.

    19. Rivers State University

    • Location: Diobu area, Port Harcourt, Rivers State
    • Year of establishment: 1980
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Excellence & Creativity

    Rivers State University has a Faculty of Law with various departments specialising in different types of law, including business, public, international, private property law, and Jurisprudence and International Law.

    18. Ekiti State University (EKSU)

    • Location: Ado Ekiti
    • Year of establishment: 1982
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Knowledge, Honour, Service

    If you are searching for the best university for lawyers, consider EKSU. This institution is known for producing competent graduates in various fields. It has a Faculty of Law with the Departments of Private and Property Law, Public Law, Business and Industrial Law, and Jurisprudence and International Law.

    17. Afe Babalola University

    • Location: Ado-Ekiti, Ekiti State
    • Year of establishment: 2009
    • Type: Private
    • Motto: Labor Servitum Et Integritas (Labor for service and Integrity)

    Afe Babalola, a Nigerian attorney and philanthropist, founded this institution in 2009. The institution has a College of Law that offers multiple undergraduate and post-graduate law and law-related programmes.

    16. The University of Calabar (UNICAL)

    • Location: Calabar, Cross River State
    • Year of establishment: 1975
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Knowledge for Service

    UNICAL has a Faculty of Law with a rich history and competent trainers and lecturers. The department was officially launched in 1980, five years after the institution was established.

    15. Olabisi Onabanjo University

    • Location: Ago-Iwoye, Ogun State
    • Year of establishment: 1982
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Excellentia Humana et Patriae opus (The Flowering of Human Abilities and Service to the Fatherland)

    14. Delta State University (DELSU)

    • Location: Abraka-Abbi Rd, Uruoka
    • Year of establishment: 1992
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Knowledge, Character and Service

    DELSU is a multi-campus state institution of higher learning. Its main campus is located in Abraka, while the other two campuses are situated in Anwai-Asaba and Oleh. The Faculty of Law is located at the Oleh campus and is one of the best law schools in the country.

    13. Ambrose Alli University

    • Location: Edo State
    • Year of establishment: 1981
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Knowledge for Advancement

    Ambrose Alli University is a state-owned institution that was formerly known as Edo State University. Its Faculty of Law has produced some of the top and most successful legal practitioners in the country.

    • Location: Uturu
    • Year of establishment: 1981
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Excellence and Service

    11. University of Maiduguri

    • Location: Maiduguri
    • Year of establishment: 1975
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Knowledge is Light

    The University of Maiduguri is one of the best higher learning institutions located in the country's northern part. The institution is particularly renowned for its Faculty of Law, which offers quality education and training.

    10. Babcock University

    • Location: Ilishan-Remo, Ogun State
    • Year of establishment: 1959
    • Type: Private
    • Motto: Knowledge, Truth, Service

    • Location: Ojo, Lagos State
    • Year of establishment: 1983
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Per la verità e di servizio (For Truth and Service)

    • Location: Jos, Plateau State
    • Year of establishment: 1975
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Discipline and Dedication

    • Location: Ile-Ife, Osun State
    • Year of establishment: 1961
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: For Learning and Culture

    • Location: Nsukka-Onitsha Rd, Nsukka
    • Year of establishment: 1955
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: To Restore the Dignity of Man

    UNN is among the first-generation federal universities founded before Nigeria's independence. Its Faculty of Law is the oldest in the country. It has made an immense contribution to legal education and practice in Nigeria.

    3. University of Ibadan (UI)

    • Location: Ibadan
    • Year of establishment: 1948
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: Recte Sapere Fons (For Knowledge and Sound Judgement)

    2. University of Lagos (UNILAG)

    • Location: Lagos
    • Year of establishment: 1962
    • Type: Public
    • Motto: In deed and in truth

    • Location: Ilorin
    • Year of establishment: 1975

    The University of Ilorin is a federal institution of higher studies in Nigeria. It is arguably the best university for lawyers in Nigeria. Its Faculty of Law inculcates in students a clear understanding of the importance of law in contemporary society.

    How many years will it take to study law in Nigeria?

    It will take you about seven years to become a qualified attorney in the country. You will spend the first five years in an accredited institution of higher learning, followed by two years at the Nigerian law school and the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC).

    Which is the cheapest university to study law in Nigeria?

    It is challenging to pick the cheapest university in the country for law. Generally, public universities are cheaper than private ones. You should look at the prospectuses of multiple institutions before choosing the most affordable one.

    What type of lawyer earns the most?
