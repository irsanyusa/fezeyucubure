Frederick Winslow Taylor (Mechanical Engineer)

05 Jul 2024, 00:00

Profession: Mechanical Engineer Biography: Frederick Winslow Taylor was an American mechanical engineer who sought to improve industrial efficiency and is known as the father of scientific management. His approach, now known as "Taylorism," revolutionized the manufacturing industry, emphasizing time studies, motion studies, and job simplification to boost productivity. Taylor's most significant work, The Principles of Scientific Management, was published in 1911 and laid out his philosophy. He argued that by scientifically studying work and determining the most efficient way to perform specific tasks, productivity could be greatly enhanced.
