Mrs. Elizabeth Reeve Cutter Morrow, widow of onetime Ambassador to Mexico and U. S. Senator Dwight Whitney Morrow, is small, dainty and a poet. Nevertheless, during the World War she organized the first U. S. women’s (relief) unit to go to France. When her late husband ran for the Senate from New Jersey she stumped the State for him. When her grandson, Charles Augustus Lindbergh Jr., was kidnapped and murdered, she was a tower of strength to her family’s morale, later stood guard over Grandson Jon Morrow Lindbergh.

Kim Petras Rewrites History by Becoming the First Transgender Woman to Win a Grammy

28 Aug 2024, 00:00

At this year’s Grammy Awards, Kim Petras and Sam Smith won the best pop duo/group performance award for their song Unholy. Petras is the first transgender woman to win this honor, and this wasn’t just a victory for talented musicians, but also a triumph for the LGBTQ+ community. In celebration of Petras making history with this win, Smith let her accept the trophy and give a speech. Chris Pizzello/Invision/East News During her heartfelt speech, Petras expressed gratitude to both pop music icon and LGBTQ+ advocate Madonna and paid homage to the late, inspiring artist SOPHIE.
